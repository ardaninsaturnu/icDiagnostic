COVID-19 IgG / IgM Test

The COVID-19 IgG / IgM GICA Rapid Test Kit can quickly detect the presence of IgG and IgM respectively, which can show patients' duration of infection. By differentiating the test results of IgG and IgM, which are very important, it can help doctors determine whether there is an early infection or a mid / late infection so that doctors can receive different treatments for these patients.

Box Contents
Test Kit 25
Steril Lancet 1
Alcohol Prep Ped 2
Diluent 1
disposable dropper 5

Product Features

More Easy :

No special equipment required, Intuitive visual interpretation.

Fast :

Quick sampling with fingertip blood, 10-minute results.

Correct :

Results with IgG and IgM, respectively, were verified using PCR and CT.

Apply :

For suspected patients with symptoms, mild symptoms, or even no symptoms, as well as testing people who have close contact with infected patients and people under quarantine control.

How to use ?

  • 1- sample collection and preparation. Keep the kit and sample at room temperature before testing.
  • 2- Add samples to the sample well using a micropipette / dropper.
  • 3- Immediately add buffer to the sample well.
  • 4- Wait for the colored line to appear and the result to be read after 10 minutes. The result is valid within 20 minutes.

How Are Test Results Interpreted ?

IgM POSITIVE: Two lines appear.

Colored lines should be in the control line region (C) and the IgM test line region. No line appears in the IgG test line region.

IgG and IgM POSITIVE: Three lines appear.

Colored lines should be in the control line region (C), the IgG line test region, and the IgM test line region. The color intensities of the lines do not have to match.

IgG POSITIVE: Two lines appear.

Colored lines should be in the control line region (C) and the IgG test line region. No line appears in the IgM test line region.

NOTE: The intensity of the color in the IgG and / or IgM test line regions will vary depending on the concentration of COVID-19 antibodies in the sample. Therefore, any shade of color in the IgG and / or IgM test line regions should be considered positive.

NEGATİVE:

One colored line should be in the control line region (C). No lines appear in the IgG and IgM test line regions.

INVALID: Control line does not appear.

Insufficient buffer volume or incorrect procedural techniques are the most likely causes of control line failure. Review the procedure and repeat the procedure with a new cassette. If the problem persists, stop using the test kit and contact your local distributor.
