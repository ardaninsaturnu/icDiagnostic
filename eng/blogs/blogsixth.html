HAS THE CORONAVIRUS CONTACT YOU?

Buesseo Seniorita
September 15, 2020

When infected with coronavirus, it shows some symptoms, but we can't say "Everyone with these symptoms has coronavirus." Because, as it is known, some symptoms such as fever and cough are the same as the symptoms of the flu. Therefore, testing is required to confirm that the individual has coronavirus. One of these tests are laboratory tests, and the other is a rapid diagnostic test.In this article, we will examine the rapid diagnostic test.

HOW IS THE RAPID DIAGNOSTIC TEST PERFORMED?

The rapid diagnostic test is divided into two: 1) Rapid antigen test: In antigen tests, a swab sample is taken from the nose or throat and virus proteins are examined. 2) Rapid antibody test: In antibody tests, the blood taken from the finger tip and immune system molecules are examined. The results of both tests are determined in a short time such as 15-20 minutes.

WHAT ARE THE ADVANTAGES OF FAST DIAGNOSTIC TEST?

It is a great advantage that the rapid diagnostic test gives results in a short time. Because this saves time and makes it possible to test more people. At the same time, while performing rapid diagnostic tests, the person can do the test himself at home without the need for experts. In this way, he does not have to go to hospitals with a very high probability of coronavirus transmission, because even if his test is negative, he can get coronavirus when he goes to the hospital. Another advantage of the rapid diagnostic test is that it is easy to carry and store due to its small size. Unlike other tests, rapid diagnostic tests detect infections in people with coronavirus even if they have little or no symptoms, so it is very important to have a rapid diagnostic test.
